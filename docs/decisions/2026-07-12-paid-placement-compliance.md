# Paid placement compliance guardrails

> Status: accepted guardrails · 2026-07-12

Okolo may sell additional prominence for events and places, but paid placement must never look
organic. The enquiry window may advertise the future service; actual boosts must not launch until
all of the following are implemented:

- Every promoted pin, card, result, recommendation and detail view carries a nearby, persistent
  text label: **„Anzeige“** in German and an equally clear localized equivalent elsewhere. Color,
  iconography or a special pin alone is not disclosure.
- The promoted detail identifies the person or business that paid for the placement.
- A directly accessible explanation of recommendation/search ranking states the main organic
  factors and the extent to which payment increases prominence.
- Advertiser terms cover payer and beneficiary identity, placement and duration, truthful claims,
  rights to supplied material, no guaranteed reach, and Okolo's right to reject or remove content.
- Advertising enquiries are handled only for the requested quote. They are not added to the
  newsletter or later marketing without consent; the privacy notice must cover enquiry retention.
- Paid prominence never alters event facts or source attribution.

The basis is Austrian [ECG §6](https://ris.bka.gv.at/NormDokument.wxe?Abfrage=Bundesnormen&Gesetzesnummer=20001703&Paragraf=6),
[MedienG §26](https://www.ris.bka.gv.at/NormDokument.wxe?Abfrage=Bundesnormen&Gesetzesnummer=10000719&Paragraf=26),
and [UWG §2 and its Annex](https://ris.bka.gv.at/NormDokument.wxe?Abfrage=Bundesnormen&Gesetzesnummer=10002665&Paragraf=2),
plus the EU [Platform-to-Business Regulation, Article 5](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX%3A32019R1150).
Reassess the [Digital Services Act, Articles 26–27](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX%3A32022R2065)
before launch if Okolo qualifies as an online platform, especially as direct organiser publishing grows.

This is a practical compliance baseline, not a substitute for Austrian legal advice before the
first paid campaign.
